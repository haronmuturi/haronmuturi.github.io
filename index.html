Profile Summary

Career Journey

My journey into civil engineering began with a childhood fascination for bridges, roads, and buildings—wondering how massive structures stand firm against nature's forces. This curiosity drove me to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT), where I graduated with Second Class Honors - Upper Division. My industrial attachments at Kenya Rural Roads Authority (KeRRA) and Meru Water & Sewerage Services (MEWASSCO) gave me my first real taste of engineering—checking levels during culvert backfilling, supervising box culvert construction, and conducting materials testing in the laboratory. These experiences taught me that engineering is not just about calculations; it's about building things that serve communities and stand the test of time.

Engineering Passion

Today, as a Civil Engineer with the State Department of Housing & Urban Development, I am actively involved in designing and supervising civil works for the Chuka Modern Market—a flagship Affordable Housing Program project. From drainage systems and road networks to water supply and structural detailing, every day brings a new challenge. I have also worked with PalmLand Surveys & Contractors Ltd, where I honed my skills in engineering surveys, data analysis, and technical reporting. Beyond the technical side, I believe engineering is a calling to solve problems, improve lives, and leave a lasting impact. Whether it's ensuring quality control on site or mentoring young engineers, I bring passion, integrity, and excellence to everything I do.
